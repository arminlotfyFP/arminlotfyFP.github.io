I have developed a diverse skill set in digital electrical engineering, with a strong focus on machine learning algorithms.

This expertise enables me to work on developing models for tasks such as data-driven prediction and decision-making. Additionally, I have gained experience in energy management strategies for electric vehicles, a field that is becoming increasingly significant as EV adoption grows.

I hold an M.Sc. in Digital Electrical Engineering from Iran University of Science and Technology and am currently pursuing a Ph.D. at Carleton University as part of the Intelligent Robotic and Energy Systems Research Group (IRES).

My research centers on the application of machine learning techniques, including reinforcement learning and deep learning, to energy management systems in electric vehicles.

I work extensively with MATLAB and Python to implement these algorithms across various EMS applications.
